Translation

*Note* Titulary is italicized, Proper Names are underlined, Places are bold.

Block 1)

Sun, Great King, Labarna, Tudhaliya, Labarna, Great King, Hero, son of Hattusili, Great King, Hero, (grandson) of Mursili, Great King, Hero

Block 2)

(I?) smote.

“I, (ethnonym)1 , Labarna, came back to the city, LINGUA+CLAVUS-tusa2

“I, Tarhuntas…

Block 3)

“…there was not,

“and when I came up to the road3,

“and…

Commentary

1I assume an ethnonym based on the suffix –wani.

2Hawkins transcribes LA+X-tu-sa, but in comparison with Payne’s sign list, I would suggest LINGUA/LA+CLAVUS-tu-sa; the meaning is still unconfirmed, but Tudhaliya is returning, possibly home, and thus the reading ‘Hattusa’ could be accepted.

3Hawkins transcribes FINES ‘border’, however the sign resembles Payne’s VIA ‘road’ more closely. The meaning is not drastically changed.
